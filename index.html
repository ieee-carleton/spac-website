About SPAC

IEEE Student Professional Awareness Conference (SPAC) is a formal dinner event that aims to bridge the gap between students and their career goals. SPAC offers students and employers a unique opportunity to network with one another in a professional environment along with professionals and academics representing the field of Engineering.

Our attendees include 175+ local students from the engineering and computer science streams at Carleton University, the University of Ottawa, and Algonquin College. Meet the brightest upcoming talent from Ottawa's leading schools.

For the last 8 years, IEEE SPAC has been organized by the IEEE Student Branches of Carleton University, University of Ottawa, and Algonquin College


Why SPAC?

SPAC will give your organization an opportunity to showcase your company’s leading technologies and products to students, researchers and academics. At SPAC your organization will network with the best and brightest engineering students in Ottawa for the possibility of a co-op work term, internship, research partnership, or future career.

Our Speakers

Take a look at who will be speaking!

Paul Vallée

Chief Executive Officer, Chairman & Founder of Pythian
Paul Vallée is an experienced entrepreneur whose passion for data and for excellence led to the foundation of Pythian in 1997. Paul started his career as a data scientist, continued as a systems architect, and by the age of 25 had started his fourth company—Pythian. From two employees and a couple of laptops, Pythian has now grown to more than 250 people with offices in North America, Europe, and Asia. As Chief Executive Officer, Paul continues to lead Pythian as a center of excellence for expert, outsourced data services for the world’s mission-critical data infrastructures.

Dr. Jim Wight

Researcher & Academic from Carleton University
A Carleton graduate himself (MEng/74 and PhD/76), Jim has supervised more than 100 graduate students, always encouraging them to build something in the real world before graduation. Over his profilic career of more than 35 years, Jim has authored or co-authored 42 refereed journal papers and 148 refereed conference papers, and has 23 issued patents. Applying his radio and electronics engineering experience to more than academia, Jim has acted as a technical advisor/program director for many private-sector companies and has pursued joint research with Defence Research & Development Canada and the Communications Research Centre.
